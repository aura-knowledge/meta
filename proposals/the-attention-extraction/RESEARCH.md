## 1. Working thesis

India's digital-access success has inverted. The same infrastructure that could have accelerated learning, production, and civic participation is now optimized to capture and monetize attention, producing measurable costs in cognition, mental health, economic output, and social trust.

## 2. Core distinction

| Layer | Access success | Attention extraction |
|---|---|---|
| Infrastructure | Cheap data, ubiquitous smartphones | Same channels now harvest attention |
| User behavior | Potential for education, production | Entertainment and social media dominate |
| Value flow | Local creation and services | Ad revenue concentrates in foreign platforms |
| Cost | Access gaps | Cognition, mental health, productivity, trust |

## 4. Claim-evidence-risk matrix

| Claim | Evidence | Risk / required rewrite |
|---|---|---|
| India has near-universal digital access but entertainment/social media crowd out education. | IAMAI-Kantar; ASER 2024. | Correlational; avoid causal attribution. |
| Ad revenue concentrates in foreign platforms. | IBEF report. | Use precise year and caveat market dynamics. |
| Short-form feeds use variable rewards, infinite scroll, autoplay, personalized recommendations. | Platform-design literature; PNAS Nexus; Yale News. | General pattern, not specific code claims. |
| Costs appear across attention, productivity, youth mental health, social trust. | PMC studies; Gallup; NCERT survey; Gloria Mark interview. | Caveat causation; use "appears" and "associated with." |
