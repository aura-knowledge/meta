## 1. Working thesis

Building substance is not about dramatic discipline or quitting the internet. It is about redirecting small windows of attention toward creation, learning, and teaching, using AI to lower the activation energy of starting.

## 2. Core distinction

| Layer | Old default | Substance-builder alternative |
|---|---|---|
| Attention use | Passive consumption in dead time | Small, repeatable acts of creation or learning |
| Friction | Starting from a blank page | AI-assisted drafts, explanations, and prototypes |
| Cost | Equipment, courses, schedule changes | Redirection of existing attention |
| Progress signal | Likes, views, finished products | Skill stack built through reps and teaching |

## 4. Claim-evidence-risk matrix

| Claim | Evidence | Risk / required rewrite |
|---|---|---|
| Substance is built through small, repeated acts. | Ericsson's deliberate-practice research; Newport's writing on deep work. | Avoid implying any rep count guarantees expertise. |
| AI lowers activation energy for starting. | Duolingo Max; Khanmigo; general AI-assistance examples. | Keep examples practical, not product-pitchy. |
| The main cost is redirecting attention, not money. | Behavioral habit research; minimal-equipment examples. | Respect readers with genuinely constrained resources. |
| Unfinished early projects still build the skill stack. | Deliberate-practice literature; anecdotal creator biographies. | Frame as encouragement, not an excuse to avoid finishing. |
| Teaching turns passive information into owned knowledge. | Learning-by-teaching research (common knowledge in education literature). | Cite a public source if possible before publication. |
