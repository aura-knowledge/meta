## 1. Working thesis

India has a narrow generational window: young population, deep software talent, and universal AI-tool access. But the attention economy trained a generation to consume before it creates. The next few years will determine whether India builds the AI age or merely scrolls through it.

## 2. Core distinction

| Lens | Consumption path | Creation path |
|---|---|---|
| Individual habit | Scroll, watch, react | Write, code, design, teach, translate |
| Economic output | Aggregator platforms, convenience commerce | Deep-tech products, IP, tools |
| National capacity | Young but distracted | Young and producing |

## 4. Claim-evidence-risk matrix

| Claim | Evidence | Risk / required rewrite |
|---|---|---|
| AI has collapsed the cost of individual creation. | OECD; AI Index; general AI tooling trends. | Avoid overstating quality or removing all expertise requirements. |
| The attention economy trained users to consume. | ASER 2024; BCG creator economy; attention-economy literature. | Correlational, not causal. |
| Creator-economy incentives reward reach over substance. | BCG report; platform-design critiques. | Platform-specific; avoid universal claims. |
| Small daily attention choices may compound into national capacity. | Interpretive claim; use historical analogies cautiously. | Frame as opportunity-cost argument, not deterministic prediction. |
