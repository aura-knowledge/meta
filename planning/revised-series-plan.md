## Goal and non-goals

**Goal:** Turn the four published seed articles into a deep, self-contained research series that:
- Keeps every article readable on its own.
- Uses data visualizations and charts to make the evidence concrete.
- Never uses internal lane/arc labels in article text; cross-references use article titles and links.
- Covers the problem, the AI opportunity cost, individual practice, and systemic design in enough depth that a reader does not need to look elsewhere.

**Non-goals:**
- Not a product pitch or a startup business plan (a final synthesis article may sketch product alternatives, but the series itself remains analytical).
- Not a single academic report; it is a public-facing essay series.
- Not a moral lecture on users; the frame is structural design and opportunity cost.

## Visualization strategy

### Chart types by purpose
- **Bar charts** for comparisons (platform share, time use, engagement, income distribution).
- **Line charts** for trends (Tele-MANAS calls, short-form user growth, screen-time growth).
- **Stacked bars / treemaps** for composition (daily online time by activity, ad revenue by platform).
- **Timelines** for historical and regulatory arcs.
- **Flow diagrams** for engagement loops, AI-assisted workflows, and platform incentives (Mermaid).
- **Network/edge diagrams** for content amplification, federation, and accountability ecosystems (Mermaid).
- **Before/after conceptual diagrams** for public spaces, family tables, and design alternatives (Mermaid or SVG).
- **Heatmaps** for observational data (phone use by location/time).
- **Lorenz-curve-style charts** for inequality (creator income, attention concentration).
- **Radar charts** for comparing alternative metrics or unresolved tensions.
- **Scatter plots** for country-level comparisons (AI readiness vs. attention metrics).

### Implementation
1. **Mermaid** for conceptual diagrams, timelines, flowcharts, and simple network graphs. Site already depends on Mermaid.
2. **Python + matplotlib** for data-heavy charts, exported as SVG. Charts live in `public/images/articles/2026/attention-substance-ai-moment/` and are referenced from article markdown.
3. Each Python chart is generated from a CSV in `scripts/charts/data/` so the figure is reproducible and the source data is version-controlled.
4. Every chart has alt text and a caption that cites the source.

### Chart pipeline
- Directory: `scripts/charts/` with `generate.py`, `data/*.csv`, and per-chart config entries.
- Run: `python3 scripts/charts/generate.py` regenerates all SVGs.
- New charts are added by creating a CSV and a small config entry; no new dependencies beyond matplotlib.
